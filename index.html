How Ranked Choice Voting works

1

Voters rank candidates

Instead of choosing one person, voters order their preferences: 1st choice, 2nd choice, 3rd, and so on.

2

First-choice votes are tallied

The initial count looks just like a regular election. If any candidate has more than 50% of votes, they win immediately.

3

Last-place candidate is eliminated

If no majority exists, the candidate in last place is removed from the race entirely.

4

Votes are redistributed

Everyone who ranked the eliminated candidate #1 has their vote transferred to whoever they ranked #2. This repeats until someone has a majority.
